# Historical Divergence Rules

1. Fixed historical events occur on historical rails; choices change the player's response and consequences, not the recorded event.
2. Plausible mode permits institutional concessions or organizational survival only when accumulated resources, relationships, and prior choices support them.
3. Unbound mode may unlock major divergence, but still requires causal prerequisites and labels counterfactual outcomes.
4. No character receives knowledge of later purges, Stalin's victory, the USSR's later form, or Lenin's death.
5. The state remains Soviet Russia/RSFSR in 1921; republic status is modeled separately.
6. Invented dialogue, meetings, votes, and organizers are internally marked `historically_plausible` or `fictional_composite`.
7. Generated or illustrated historical imagery must be labeled reconstruction, never photograph.
8. Simulation numbers communicate estimates and uncertainty, not documentary fact.
