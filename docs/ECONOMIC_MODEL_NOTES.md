# Economic Model Notes

National conditions use normalized 0–100 indices rather than false-precision historical units. The chapter starts with collapsed industry/agriculture, scarce grain/urban food, high inflation/black-market activity, damaged railways, famine, disease, corruption, and exhaustion.

Regional output, food supply, famine, and infrastructure feed map modes and event/operation choices. Monthly rules currently worsen disease during severe famine and connect exposure to security/repression. NEP, grain policy, private trade, labor discipline, union autonomy, and factory committees define multi-system policy effects.

Future calibration should add population-weighted aggregation, explicit budget flows, railway bottlenecks, price indices, and transparent monthly formulas. Current values are gameplay estimates, not statistical claims.
