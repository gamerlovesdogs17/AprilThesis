# Factions and Ideologies

The Workers' Opposition is modeled as a coalition, not a single ideology. Eight internal blocs expose union syndicalism, Bolshevik loyalty, economic pragmatism, underground preservation, anti-bureaucratic coalition building, socialist feminism, factory militancy, and regional survival. Each has a leader, support, satisfaction, preferences, red lines, willingness to work underground, relationship notes, and split risk. Monthly drift and limited faction actions make the coalition an active management problem.

Regional influence also distinguishes the Central Committee, Secretariat apparatus, Trotsky-aligned networks, Zinoviev's organization, Left Communists, Democratic Centralists, underground Socialist Revolutionaries/Mensheviks, and anarchists. These fields represent estimated political reach, never sovereignty.

The blocs are fictional composites designed to represent documented tensions without claiming an archival internal-caucus roster. Future work can add conference and merger mechanics after stronger sourcing.
