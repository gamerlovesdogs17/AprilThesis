# Factions and Ideologies

The Workers' Opposition is modeled as a coalition, not a single ideology. The current campaign exposes union syndicalism, Bolshevik loyalty, economic pragmatism, underground preservation, anti-bureaucratic coalition building, socialist feminism, and factory militancy through event choices and the cohesion/resource model.

Regional influence also distinguishes the Central Committee, Secretariat apparatus, Trotsky-aligned networks, Zinoviev's organization, Left Communists, Democratic Centralists, underground Socialist Revolutionaries/Mensheviks, and anarchists. These fields represent estimated political reach, never sovereignty.

Future work should turn the implicit blocs into named internal caucuses with leaders, agendas, red lines, conferences, splits, and merger mechanics.
