# Regional Geography

The campaign keeps 28 stable strategic composites for simulation, while the default selectable geography is a March 1921 historical-GIS reconstruction. Strategic shapes are generated by dissolving their member provinces and never clip those provinces.

## Historical geography

The IISH/RiStat 1897 province and uyezd GeoPackages provide the real vector foundation. Machine-readable dated rules retain, split, transfer, merge, or rename those source features into 96 records, of which 93 are active in March. Formal-government boundaries and strategic aggregates are derived dissolves. Detailed uncertainties are listed province by province in `PROVINCE_RECONSTRUCTION_1921.md`.

Every selectable province has a stable simulation-parent mapping. That relationship transfers current political state to the map without altering province geometry. The 28 older hand-authored strategic polygons remain only as compatibility inputs for legacy simulation helpers; they are not default visible surfaces.

## Physical and local geography

National rivers use Natural Earth 1:50m centerlines, with a checksum-pinned OSM relation supplement for the Moskva River. Railways are documented small-scale route digitizations checked against the 1921 and 1922 reference maps. Both are clipped and attributed province by province. Cities and historical sites use geographic coordinates and explicit province IDs.

## Limits

Whole-uyezd reconstruction cannot express every treaty, canton, occupation, or sub-district line. The western frontiers, Mountain and Bashkir internal divisions, Transcaucasian disputes, and Sakhalin occupation context retain explicit uncertainty. The 24 site records distinguish documented locations from historically plausible composites.
