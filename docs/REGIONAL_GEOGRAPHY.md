# Regional Geography

The 28 selectable areas are strategic gameplay composites spanning the RSFSR, Soviet republics, borderlands, and the Far Eastern theater. They are not modern Russian federal subjects and do not claim archival boundary precision.

Each tracks population (in thousands), cities, economic role, composition, administration, faction influence, peasant/national movements, soviet/union/factory organization, Cheka and Red Army presence, output, food/famine, infrastructure, unrest, worker support, and intelligence confidence.

Known simplifications: Kharkov is grouped with the Donbas industrial theater; the Baltic Frontier is an operational border zone; Siberian and Central Asian spaces are heavily aggregated; the Caucasus is enlarged for selection; Georgia's March description reflects the newly Sovietized republic after the invasion rather than continued independence.
