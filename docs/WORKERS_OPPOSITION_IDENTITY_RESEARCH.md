# Workers’ Opposition Identity Research

## Conclusion

The reviewed archival and catalog records did not establish a verified independent Workers’ Opposition emblem. That is a bounded research finding, not proof that no symbol was ever used. The former invented circular insignia was therefore removed from identity-bearing UI.

`WO` survives only as an explicitly neutral interface abbreviation. The faction is represented through its full name, leaders, institutional base, platform language, and a documentary facsimile of Alexandra Kollontai’s 1921 *The Workers Opposition in Russia*.

## Documentary asset

The local title-page image comes from the Wikimedia Commons scan of the 1921 English IWW edition, whose repository record points to Internet Archive item `case_hd_8055_i4_r67_box_004`. The Commons record marks the work public domain in the United States and notes that the author died more than 70 years ago.

Metadata records exact title, author form, publication year, publisher, language, source, original repository, rights, modifications, historical date, availability, and `documentaryOnly: true`. Because the exact 1921 publication month was not established, the UI does not pretend that this edition was physically present in a March 1921 room.

Kollontai’s 1923 portrait remains available only with its date and documentary-only label. Shliapnikov’s period photograph is separately identified. Medvedev retains text treatment rather than an uncertain portrait.
